# Software-Development-Lifecycle

How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating "user stories" help with this?
  Everyone has something that they expect when interacting with a piece of software, and what they expect differs from person to person. Given the type of software being used though, there are commonalities that all anticipated users can reasonably be provided with. When designing a program, I need to put myself into the role of the end user. How might I expect to access certain menus or functions? Are there ways that I might not expect, that when used break the program or cause bugs? These are the questions I need to be asking myself.

  How do I approach developing programs? What agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
    The agile concepts of a daily Scrum Standup and Information Radiator are beneficial concepts that enhance a team's ability to work together and work efficiently. Maintaining constant lines of communication serves to improve the team's workflows and total output.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
  Just like in any other field, a good team member provides useful input and suggestions to their teammmates while also keeping an open mind to suggestions themselves. Egos have no place in a well-functioning team.
